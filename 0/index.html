Part 1: Selfie: The Wrong Way vs. The Right Way

On the left, we have a distorted "selfie" photo, and on the right, we have a far-away zoomed-in photo.

We can think of the perspective of the camera as a cone of vision. Objects near the tip of the cone (close to the camera) will take up a significant portion of the field of view and appear significantly larger. This is why features such as the nose are enlarged and the ears are hidden. Further away, this effect is diminished since the ratio of object to cone of vision is not changing as dramatically.
